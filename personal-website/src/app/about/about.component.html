I'm interested in a lot of things. In particular, I'm driven to learn about learning. That's why web application development is particularly interesting to me: there's quite a few ways to solve any given problem.

About Me

When I was a kid, my mother used to take me to the library at least once a week. I loved being at the library. From the time I met the local librarian and started to get book reccomendations from her, I wanted to become a librarian. By the time I entered high school I was planning out my career, volunteering at the school library and asking every librarian I encountered what undergraduate major I should choose before entering an MLS program. Most gave an answer that surprised me: computer information science. "You've got to learn databases" was the takeaway; that's just the way things are going.

My love for databases and data derailed those early career plans, at least for the moment. As I learned about normalization, DBMS and SQL, I started to think about data, its organization and how it can be used to solve problems. The most interesting problems to me are still about mostly related to research: how can we sort through massive amounts of data and information to find what is useful to us? How do we ask the right questions and know what will be useful to us? How is learning and research changing in its inevitable proximity an ever-expanding internet?

Why I Made This Site

Learning about software, systems and data has become a practice of actively engaging and considering these questions. It allows me to consider learning in a deeper way than what I used to consider 'studying' and incorporate visual, experiencial and technological aspects of learning by trial and error.

This website is designed to organize my projects and display snapshots my recent work and ongoing projects. I want to maintain a centralized location for the things I'm learning about, so it will likely be redesigned frequently. Although my primary subject of study is web application development and technology, I am also interested in visual art, design, literature, philosophy, economics, history and music. I hope this page might spark conversation and collaboration and invite you to contact me if anything you see speaks to your interests.
